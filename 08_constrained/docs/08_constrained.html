Constraints and the Tangent Plane

Constraints

General nonlinear programming problems are of the form

minimizef(ùê±)subject toùê°(ùê±)=ùüé,ùê†(ùê±)‚â•ùüé,ùê±‚ààŒ©. \begin{align} \operatorname{minimize} & f(\bm{x}) \\ \text{subject to} & \bm{h}(\bm{x}) = \bm{0}, \;\; \bm{g}(\bm{x}) \geq \bm{0}, \\ & \bm{x} \in \Omega. \end{align}

  • An inequality constraint is said to be active at ùê±\bm{x} if gi(ùê±)=0g_i(\bm{x}) = 0.
  • It is said to be inactive if gi(ùê±)>0g_i(\bm{x}) > 0.
  • Any equality constraint hi(ùê±)=0h_i(\bm{x}) = 0 is active.
  • In the figure, g1g_1 is active, g2g_2 and g3g_3 are not.
  • If it were known a priori which constraints were active at an optimal solution then it would be a local minimum point of the problem defined by ignoring the inactive constraints.
  • ùê°=(h1,h2,‚Ä¶,hm)\bm{h} = (h_1, h_2, \ldots, h_m), ùê†=(g1,g2,‚Ä¶,gp)\;\;\bm{g} = (g_1, g_2, \ldots, g_p) are functional constraints.
  • ùê±‚ààŒ©\bm{x} \in \Omega: set constraint.
  • We will therefore start by ignoring the inequality constraints and come back to them later.

Lemma

Let ùê±*\bm{x}^\ast be a regular point of the constraints ùê°(ùê±)=ùüé\bm{h}(\bm{x}) = \bm{0} and a local extremum point of ff subject to these constraints. Let ùêù‚ààTùê±*S\bm{d} \in T_{\bm{x}^\ast}S and let ùê±(t)‚ààS\bm{x}(t) \in S such that ùê±(0)=ùê±*\bm{x}(0) = \bm{x}^\ast and ùê±Ãá(0)=ùêù\dot{\bm{x}}(0) = \bm{d} for ‚àía‚â§t‚â§a-a \leq t \leq a for some a>0a > 0.

Theorem (FONC)

Let ùê±*\bm{x}^\ast be a regular local minimum point of ff subject to the constraint ùê°(ùê±)=ùüé\bm{h}(\bm{x}) = \bm{0}. Then there is a ùõå‚àà‚Ñùm\bm{\lambda} \in \mathbb{R}^m such that

‚àáf(ùê±*)‚àíùõå‚ä§‚àáùê°(ùê±*)=ùüé.(1) \nabla f(\bm{x}^\ast) - \bm{\lambda}^\top \nabla \bm{h}(\bm{x}^\ast) = \bm{0}. \qquad(1)

Proof

From the lemma, we may conclude that the linear system

‚àáf(ùê±*)ùêù‚â†0,and‚àáùê°(ùê±*)ùêù=ùüé \nabla f(\bm{x}^\ast) \bm{d} \neq 0, \;\; \text{and} \;\; \nabla \bm{h}(\bm{x}^\ast)\bm{d} = \bm{0}

Has no feasible solution ùêù\bm{d}. Then, by Farkas‚Äôs lemma, its alternative system must have a solution. Specifically, there is a ùõå‚àà‚Ñùm\bm{\lambda} \in \mathbb{R}^m such that ‚àáf(ùê±*)‚àíùõå‚ä§‚àáùê°(ùê±*)=ùüé\nabla f(\bm{x}^\ast) - \bm{\lambda}^\top \nabla \bm{h}(\bm{x}^\ast) = \bm{0}.

The FONC Equation 1 together with the constraints ùê°(ùê±*)=ùüé\bm{h}(\bm{x}^\ast) = \bm{0} give a total of n+mn+m equations in the n+mn+m variables comprising ùê±*,ùõå\bm{x}^\ast, \bm{\lambda}.

  • Introduce the Lagrangian associated with the constrained problem, defined as

‚Ñì(ùê±,ùõå)=f(ùê±)‚àíùõå‚ä§ùê°(ùê±).(2) \ell(\bm{x}, \bm{\lambda}) = f(\bm{x}) - \bm{\lambda}^\top \bm{h}(\bm{x}). \qquad(2)

‚àáùê±‚Ñì(ùê±,ùõå)=ùüé,‚àáùõå‚Ñì(ùê±,ùõå)=ùüé.(3) \nabla_{\bm{x}} \ell(\bm{x}, \bm{\lambda}) = \bm{0}, \qquad \nabla_{\bm{\lambda}} \ell(\bm{x}, \bm{\lambda}) = \bm{0}. \qquad(3)

    • Each Œªi\lambda_i is the penalty weight on equality constraint hi(ùê±)=0h_i(\bm{x}) = 0.
    • With appropriate Œªi\lambda_i‚Äôs, a constrained problem could then be solved as an unconstrained optimization problem.
    • If ff is convex and ùê°(ùê±)\bm{h}(\bm{x}) is affine ùêÄùê±‚àíùêõ\bm{Ax} - \bm{b}, then ‚Ñì(‚ãÖ)\ell(\cdot) is convex in ùê±\bm{x} for every fixed ùõå\bm{\lambda}.
